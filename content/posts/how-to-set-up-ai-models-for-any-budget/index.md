Because [SEO Content Architect](https://content-architect.ns5.club) is a BYOK (bring-your-own-key) app, you don't buy a fixed "quality tier" — you dial cost against quality yourself, per article, by choosing which text and image models run the job. The same pipeline can produce an article for **a few cents** or for **$1–2**, and the difference is entirely which models you point it at. This guide gives you three ready-made setups — Budget, Balanced, and Premium — so you can match every article to what it's actually worth.

## The One Rule Before Any Model Choice: Set Length to "Long"

Whatever your budget, set the article length to **Long**. This isn't a cost lever — it's a quality floor. Short and medium settings tend to produce thin, surface-level content that Google's helpful-content systems treat as low-value, and thin articles rarely rank regardless of how good the model is. A longer target gives the model room to cover the topic properly, hit more long-tail queries, and support a real FAQ and internal-link structure.

Length affects cost only marginally — output tokens are cheap. **Model choice, not length, is where your per-article price is decided.** So: Long for everything, then pick your models by tier below. Keep [High Quality Mode](publish-first-ai-article-to-wordpress-in-10-minutes.md) and keyword research on as well.

## Tier 1 — Budget: A Few Cents per Article

The goal here is decent, publishable content at the lowest possible cost — ideal for high-volume niches, programmatic content, or testing a new site before you invest in it.

- **Text model: GPT-4o mini.** Extremely cheap per token with genuinely decent quality for standard informational articles. It won't have the editorial flair of a frontier model, but on the Long setting with keyword research on, it produces solid, rank-able drafts.
- **Image model: P-Image (Replicate).** A cost-optimized image model that delivers decent visuals for a fraction of the price of premium generators. Perfect for illustrative, decorative, or supporting images where you don't need photorealism or embedded text.

**Realistic cost: a few cents per fully-illustrated article.** This is the setup for anyone who cares most about [cost per article at scale](real-cost-of-ai-generated-articles-byok-vs-saas.md).

## Tier 2 — Balanced: The Best Value in the App

This is the tier most publishers should live in. You give up almost nothing on quality but pay a fraction of what "maximum" costs.

- **Text model: Claude Sonnet 4.6.** The single best price-to-quality ratio for long-form writing in the app. Sonnet produces the most natural, human-sounding prose with the least [de-AI-ification editing](how-to-write-seo-articles-with-ai-without-google-penalties.md), while costing far less than the top frontier models. For most blogs, this is the sweet spot.
- **Image model: Flux 2 Pro** (for images without text). Strong image quality at a much lower price than the premium option. As long as the image doesn't need readable words baked into it, Flux 2 Pro looks great and keeps costs down.

**When to reach past this tier:** only for money pages, pillar guides, or any image that must contain legible text. Everything else, Balanced handles beautifully.

## Tier 3 — Premium: Maximum Quality, $1–2 per Article

When an article is worth investing in — a pillar page, a conversion-critical landing article, flagship editorial — spend on it deliberately.

- **Text model: the strongest models via OpenRouter.** One OpenRouter key unlocks hundreds of models, including every frontier model. For maximum quality, point the pipeline at a top-tier model (the most powerful Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google options) and let it do its best work. See [how the model families compare](gemini-vs-gpt-vs-claude-best-ai-model-for-writing.md).
- **Image model: Nano Banana 2.** The highest image quality in the app, and — critically — **multilingual and reliable with text inside the image.** If your image needs a headline, a label, a diagram caption, or any readable words, Nano Banana 2 is not optional, it's mandatory. No other model in the stack renders in-image text as cleanly, in as many languages.

**Realistic cost: roughly $1–2 per article** with a top Anthropic (or equivalent) text model plus Nano Banana 2 imagery. Reserve this tier for the articles that earn it.

## The Text-in-Image Rule (Don't Skip This)

Two clean rules cover every image decision:

1. **Image needs readable text inside it?** → **Nano Banana 2, always.** Infographics, quote cards, labeled diagrams, anything with words. It's multilingual, so this holds whether you publish in English, Romanian, or any other language.
2. **Image is purely visual (no text)?** → **Flux 2 Pro** for high quality at a lower price, or **P-Image** if you're optimizing hard for cost.

Getting this one rule right saves the most common regeneration cycle: paying for a cheap image, discovering the embedded text is garbled, and redoing it on Nano Banana 2 anyway.

## FAQ

**Which tier should I start with?**
Balanced (Claude Sonnet 4.6 + Flux 2 Pro, Long setting). It's the best value and produces work you can publish with a light edit. Drop to Budget for high-volume niches, jump to Premium only for your most important pages.

**Is GPT-4o mini really good enough to publish?**
For standard informational articles, yes — especially on the Long setting with keyword research on and a human editing pass. It won't match Sonnet's prose, but it ranks. Test it on your niche; at a few cents per article, trying it costs almost nothing.

**When is Nano Banana 2 worth the premium?**
Any time the image contains readable text — infographics, quote cards, labeled visuals — it's the only reliable choice, and it's multilingual. For purely decorative images, Flux 2 Pro or P-Image save money with no visible downside.

**Do I need all these API keys?**
You only need keys for the providers you use, and each is free to create with pay-as-you-go pricing. A practical starter set is one OpenRouter key (which alone covers most text and image models) plus a Replicate key if you want the cheapest images via P-Image. See the [BYOK cost breakdown](real-cost-of-ai-generated-articles-byok-vs-saas.md).

**Can I mix tiers in one batch?**
Yes — model choice is per generation, so a single publishing pipeline can run Budget, Balanced, and Premium articles in the same session. Match each article to what it's worth.
