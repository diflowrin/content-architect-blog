**Content automation** is the practice of letting software handle the repetitive parts of publishing — sourcing topics, writing and illustrating articles, scheduling posts, and distributing them to social platforms — so you can focus on strategy and quality control instead of manual busywork.

On the SEO Content Architect blog, the *Automation* tag collects practical, hands-on guides for building content pipelines that actually run on their own. The goal is never automation for its own sake: it's about removing the bottlenecks that keep a blog from publishing consistently, while keeping the guardrails that stop automated output from turning into low-value spam.

A complete automation setup usually covers five stages:

- **Topic sourcing** — pulling fresh ideas from RSS feeds, news APIs, trending data, or keyword lists.
- **Generation** — using AI models to draft SEO-structured articles with keywords, headings, and FAQ sections.
- **Illustration** — creating featured and in-article images automatically.
- **Publishing** — sending finished articles to WordPress via the REST API, on a schedule.
- **Distribution** — repurposing each article into social posts, carousels, and short videos.
